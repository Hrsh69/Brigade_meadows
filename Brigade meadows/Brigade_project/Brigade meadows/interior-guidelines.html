Interior Work Policy

1. Work Timings

  • Interior work is strictly permitted only between 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM on working days.
  • No work is allowed on Sundays and public holidays.
  • Violation of timings (e.g., drilling or hammering after hours) will lead to power disconnection without further notice.

2. Labor Stay and Conduct

  • Laborers must not stay inside flats beyond 6:00 PM under any circumstance.
  • Workers are not allowed to loiter or use common amenities during or after work hours.
  • Chewing tobacco or spitting in premises is strictly prohibited.
  • Smoking is not allowed inside units or common areas — the society is a No Smoking Zone.

3. Worker Identification & Contractor Documentation

  • All workers must carry valid ID (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, or Driving License).
  • Contractors must submit:
    • Photo ID proof and photographs of all laborers
    • A copy of the Work Permit to security at Gate A or B

4. Common Area Usage Restrictions

  • Work must be strictly confined to the designated flat.
  • No items, tools, debris, or materials should block staircases, lifts, or corridors.
  • Workers are not allowed to tamper with common-area water valves or electrical boards.
  • Use of passenger lifts for moving heavy materials or during move-in/move-out is prohibited — only service lifts or stairs must be used.

5. Debris & Waste Disposal

  • Contractors and owners are fully responsible for proper disposal of interior debris or leftover construction material.
  • Housekeeping staff will not collect renovation debris or accidental construction waste.
  • No waste should be dumped in corridors, near lifts, or public areas.

6. Advance Notice & Formalities

  • All interior work formalities must be completed at least one day in advance.
  • Permission must be obtained from the Estate Office and relevant documents submitted.

7. Security Deposit Policy

  • A refundable caution deposit of ₹15,000 (by cheque only) is mandatory before starting interior work.
  • Cheque should be payable to: "Brigade Meadows Apartment Owners Association Phase-1".
  • This will only be returned after inspection, subject to no damage to common infrastructure.

8. Rules Linked with Penalties

  • Unauthorized work beyond allowed hours (6:00 PM) or on holidays will attract power disconnection and fines.
  • Any damage to lifts, walls, or shared infrastructure (due to negligence) will attract critical penalties.
  • Using common areas as temporary storerooms, drilling in shared walls, or using society electricity is punishable.
  • Misuse of lifts or staining corridors will attract cleaning penalties or service charge deductions.

9. Electrical/Plumbing Work Specifics

  • Only licensed electricians or plumbers should perform modification work inside flats.
  • Switching off power/water for the building for private interior work is not permitted without prior written permission.

10. Noise and Nuisance Control

  • Contractors and workers must avoid loud machinery use or drilling during rest hours (especially before 9 AM or after 6 PM).
  • Persistent complaints from neighbors regarding dust, sound, or debris will lead to immediate suspension of work.

11. Legal and Safety Compliance

  • All interior works must comply with BBMP/local authority guidelines and fire safety regulations.
  • Unauthorized modifications (e.g., structural changes, balcony enclosures) are prohibited and subject to legal action.

12. Responsibilities of Residents & Owners

  • Owners are responsible for contractor behavior and restoration of damage caused to society property.
  • If tenants are performing interior work, the owner must coordinate and remain accountable for rule compliance.
