Pet Policy

    1. General Guidelines

    • Residents are allowed to keep pets, provided they comply with society rules and applicable local laws.
    • All pet owners must register their pets with the society office, providing details such as breed, vaccination records, and contact information.
    • Exotic or dangerous animals (as per local laws) are not permitted in the society premises.

    2. Pet Owner Responsibilities

    • Pet owners must ensure that their pets do not cause a nuisance to other residents through excessive noise, aggressive behavior, or foul odor.
    • Pets should always be kept on a leash or in a carrier when in common areas. Free-roaming of pets is strictly prohibited.
    • Pet owners should carry a poop scooper when in common areas.
    • Pet owners must clean up after their pets and dispose of waste in designated pet waste bins. Failure to do so may result in fines or penalties.
    • Pet feeding in common areas, corridors, or near staircases is not allowed. Feeding should only take place inside the owner's premises or in designated pet-friendly zones (if any).
    • Pets are not allowed in children's play areas, swimming pools, clubhouses, sports areas or other restricted zones as specified by the society.

    3. Health & Safety Regulations

    • All pets must be vaccinated as per veterinary guidelines, and proof of vaccination must be submitted to the society office annually or at regular intervals as per norms.
    • If a pet bites a resident, guest, or staff member, the owner is fully responsible for medical expenses and any legal implications.
    • If a pet is found to be unwell and poses a health risk to others, the society may request the owner to take necessary medical action.

    4. Noise & Disturbance Control

    • Pet owners must ensure that their pets do not create excessive noise, especially during quiet hours (e.g., 10 PM – 7 AM).
    • Continuous complaints from neighbors about noise or disturbances may result in warnings or action from the society management including fines as applicable.

    5. Complaint Resolution

    • Any pet-related complaints should be formally submitted to the society office.
    • The Executive Committee will review complaints and, if necessary, issue warnings or take further action.
    • If repeated violations occur, penalties may be imposed as per society bye-laws.

Solution to Stray Dogs
  • What is a Stray Dog?
    • Dogs without an owner or permanent shelter.
    • Commonly found in public spaces: streets, parks, school and hospital areas, railway and bus stations.
    • They survive by scavenging food waste and living in open areas.
  • Legal Aspects:
    • It is a criminal offense to harm, kill, or relocate stray dogs (ABC Rules 2001).
    • Only humane management is allowed; removal or violence is strictly prohibited.
    • Residents and associations must comply with these laws to avoid legal consequences.
  • How Are Stray Dogs Controlled?
    • ABC-ARV Programme:
      • Dogs are humanely caught by trained personnel.
      • Sterilization prevents further breeding (no more puppies).
      • Anti-rabies vaccination protects both dogs and humans.
      • De-worming improves dog health and reduces disease risk.
      • After treatment, dogs are released back to their original location.
      • Managed dogs are marked with a V-shaped notch on one ear.
    • Waste Management:
      • Proper disposal of food and meat waste is crucial.
      • Do not dump leftovers in open areas; use closed bins.
      • Less food waste means fewer stray dogs in the area.
  • Dog-Related Complaints:
    • Contact BBMP control rooms in your zone for immediate action.
    • Reach out to Animal Welfare NGOs working with BBMP for rescue or sterilization.
    • If issues persist, escalate to the Deputy Director (Animal Husbandry), BBMP.
    • Find more details and contacts at www.bbmp.gov.in.
  • Why Do Stray Dogs Get Aggressive?
    • Sexual aggression during mating season.
    • Maternal aggression when protecting puppies.
    • Food aggression when competing for food.
    • Fear aggression if threatened or chased.
    • Territorial aggression to protect familiar areas.
    • Rabies aggression: sick dogs may attack without reason.
  • How to Prevent Dog Attacks:
    • Avoid packs of dogs, especially during mating or nursing periods.
    • Do not stare at, chase, or throw objects at dogs.
    • Do not run if approached by a dog; stay calm and back away slowly.
    • Avoid carrying food openly in areas with stray dogs.
    • Be alert in areas where dogs are known to live.
    • Recognize signs of rabid dogs: excessive drooling, confusion, unprovoked aggression.
  • Community Responsibility:
    • Support and participate in sterilization and vaccination drives.
    • Educate children and residents about safe and respectful behavior around dogs.
    • Discourage feeding strays in public/common areas; if feeding, do so responsibly and clean up after.
    • Report aggressive, sick, or unsterilized dogs to authorities—do not attempt to handle them yourself.
    • Encourage neighbors to use closed bins for food waste and not to litter.
  • Identifying Managed Strays:
    • Look for a V-shaped notch on one ear, indicating the dog has been sterilized and vaccinated.
    • Managed dogs are less likely to be aggressive and help keep new, unsterilized dogs away.
  • What to Do If Bitten by a Dog:
    • Wash the wound immediately with soap and water for at least 10 minutes.
    • Seek medical attention and get anti-rabies vaccination as soon as possible.
    • Report the incident to local authorities for follow-up action.
  • Benefits of Humane Stray Dog Management:
    • Reduces stray dog population over time.
    • Prevents rabies and other diseases.
    • Creates a safer and more harmonious community for both people and animals.
  • Key Contacts:
    • BBMP Control Room: For immediate complaints and emergencies.
    • Animal Welfare NGOs: For rescue, sterilization, and vaccination support.
    • Deputy Director (Animal Husbandry), BBMP: For unresolved or escalated issues.
